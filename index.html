IS THE WORLD IMPROVING?

A Look at Child Mortality Rates

Twenty-Five years ago, the Convention on the Rights of the Child brought the problem of child-mortality to the world's attention. Since 1990, many changes have been made, but the Millenium Development Goal - to reduce the mortality rate of children under five years of age by two-thirds between 1990 and 2015 - has still not been achieved. However, there has still been significant progress and the global under-five mortality rate has fallen by 53 percent since 1990.

Source: UNICEF, "Committing to Child Survival: A Promise Renewed (Progress Report 2015)"

Child Mortality
One of the Millenium Development Goals established in 2000 is to reduce child mortality across the world. In September 25, 2015 a set 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) were adopted by the United Nations' world leaders and will continue to drive progress. (Source: UNDP)
The number of under-five deaths has declined from about 12.7 million in 1990 to around 5.9 million in 2015. (Source: UNICEF)
Some countries have low child mortality rates...
Luxembourg has the lowest Child Mortality Rate in 2015 with 1.9 child deaths per 1,000 live births.
...while other countries have much higher child mortality rates.
Angola has the highest Child Mortality Rate in 2015 with 156.9 child deaths per 1,000 live births.
Sub-Saharan Africa
This region has the highest child mortality rates in the world. However, the annual rate of reduction has increased from 1.6 percent in 1990s to 4.1 percent in 2000-2015.
Sierra Leone has the highest cummulative child mortality rates from 1990-2015.
How come some countries reduce their child mortality rates more than others?
Malawi has the highest rate of change from 1990 to 2015 among the countries in Sub-Saharan Africa. The country decreased its child mortality rates by 73.6%.

Sub-Saharan Countries with the highest...

Due to the efforts in the past quarter century, many of the Sub-Saharan Countries with the highest under-five mortality rates have significantly declined. Decline in child mortality rate is the percentage decline from 1990 to 2015 in Under-five mortality in the particular country.

Malawi

Malawi has the highest decline in under-five mortality rate since 1990. It has experienced a 73.6% decline. In 1990, Malawi had the sixth highest under-five mortaltiy rate in the world at 242.4 deaths per 1,000 live child births. By 2015 it has lowered it's under-five mortality rate to 64 deaths per 1,000 live child births.

As of 2015, Malawi is no longer among the top 20 countries with the highest child mortality rates. From having the sixth highest under-five mortality rate in 1990, Malawi now has the 33rd highest under-five mortality rate.

Source: WorldBank, Last Updated: November 12, 2015, Estimates developed by the UN Inter-agency Group for Child Mortality Estimation (UNICEF, WHO, World Bank, UN DESA Population Division) at www.childmortality.org.
